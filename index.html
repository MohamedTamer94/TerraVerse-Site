About Terra

NASA's Terra satellite is a flagship Earth observing platform that has been monitoring our planet for over two decades.

Understanding Our Changing Planet

Launched in 1999, Terra was the first satellite to monitor Earth's systems with five sophisticated instruments, providing unprecedented data about our planet's climate and environment.

Terra's comprehensive observations help scientists understand how Earth's atmosphere, land, and oceans interact as a system, providing critical data for climate research and environmental management.

1999 - Launch

Terra satellite launched from Vandenberg Air Force Base, beginning its mission to observe Earth's systems

2000 - First Data

Began transmitting comprehensive Earth observation data from its five advanced instruments

2002 - Climate Records

Established baseline measurements for climate change studies that continue to this day

2020 - Extended Mission

Mission extended due to continued scientific value and ongoing contributions to Earth science

Benefits to Humanity

Terra's data provides critical insights that benefit people and ecosystems worldwide.

Climate Tracking

Monitor global temperature changes, sea level rise, and ice melt to understand and predict climate patterns.

Disaster Prediction

Track wildfires, floods, and storms to provide early warnings and improve emergency response.

Agriculture Improvement

Monitor crop health, soil moisture, and growing conditions to support food security worldwide.

Water Resource Management

Track freshwater resources, snowpack, and glacier changes to support sustainable water use.

Air Quality Monitoring

Measure pollutants and aerosols to understand air quality impacts on human health and ecosystems.

Ecosystem Conservation

Monitor deforestation, biodiversity, and habitat changes to support conservation efforts.
